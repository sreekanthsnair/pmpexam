PMP Beginner Complete Pass Guide 2025

This is your full guide to pass the 2025 PMP exam (180 Qs, 4 hrs, need ~122/175 to pass). Covers People (42%, 75 Qs), Process (50%, 90 Qs), Business (8%, 15 Qs). Half is agile (flexible projects). Learn this, practice my 500 Qs, and you’re certified!

1. People (42% - About 75 Questions)

Managing Team and People Who Care

This is about handling your team and anyone interested (stakeholders). Lots of “what’s your next step?” Qs.

  • Team Conflicts: Team fighting? Best: talk it out together (collaboration = win-win). Tight on time? Split the difference (compromise). Don’t ignore or force unless stuck.

    Ex: Two devs argue over code—get them to agree, not you decide.

  • Team Issues: Someone’s slow or messing up? First, talk privately, offer help (training, tools). Don’t fire, escalate, or add work yet.

    Ex: Designer’s late—ask why, maybe they need software tips.

  • Team Boost: Tired team? Say “great job,” give breaks, not more tasks. Happy teams work better.

    Ex: Coders hit milestone—praise them, don’t pile on.

  • Stakeholders: Anyone who cares (boss, client, team). List them (stakeholder register), rank them (power/interest grid: boss = high power), update them their way (email, calls).

    Ex: Client wants weekly texts—plan it, don’t skip.

  • Agile Teams: Flexible projects? Team picks tasks (self-organizing), you guide, don’t boss. Roles: Product Owner (sets goals), Scrum Master (helps team).

    Ex: Team chooses coding order—you just watch.

  • Tools:
    • RACI: Who does what (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed).
    • Comm Plan: Who gets what info, when (e.g., boss gets reports).

Exam Trick: Q says “conflict” or “slow”? Pick “talk/help” over “fire/escalate.”

2. Process (50% - About 90 Questions)

Making the Project Happen

Biggest part—planning, tracking, fixing. Math (EVM), agile (half the exam), risks, changes—all here.

  • 5 Steps: Start (get okay), Plan (map it), Do (build it), Check (watch it), Finish (wrap up). Every Q fits one.
  • Scope: What’s the job? Write it (charter = start doc), split into tasks (WBS = task list). Extra work? File a change request, don’t just add.

    Ex: Client wants extra app screen—request, don’t jump in.

  • Schedule: List tasks, connect them (A → B), find longest path (critical path = no delays). Speed up: add people (crashing = more $), overlap (fast-tracking = more risk).

    Ex: Design (3d) → Code (4d) = 7d critical path.

  • Money Math (EVM): Tracks if you’re on budget/time. Memorize:
    • CV = EV - AC: Done (EV) - Spent (AC). + = saving, - = over.
    • SV = EV - PV: Done - Planned (PV). + = fast, - = late.
    • CPI = EV / AC: >1 = cheap, <1 = pricey.
    • SPI = EV / PV: >1 = ahead, <1 = behind.
    • EAC = BAC / CPI: Total Budget (BAC) / CPI = final cost guess.
    • TCPI = (BAC - EV) / (BAC - AC): Efficiency to hit budget.

    Ex: EV = $50K, AC = $60K, CV = -$10K (over), CPI = 0.83 (bad).

  • Risks: Bad stuff might happen. List them (register), rate them (chance × cost = EMV), fix them (avoid = dodge, mitigate = lessen, transfer = give away, accept = deal). Hits? Use your fix.

    Ex: 40% chance of $20K delay = $8K EMV, plan to lessen.

  • Agile (40-50 Qs): Short bursts (sprints = 2-4 weeks), daily chats (15 min), team picks from list (backlog, Product Owner ranks it). No mid-sprint adds—next sprint only.
    • Roles: Product Owner (goals), Scrum Master (helps), Team (builds).
    • Events: Plan sprint, daily check, show work (review), fix process (retro).

    Ex: Boss wants feature mid-sprint—say no, backlog it.

  • Changes: Any tweak (scope, time, budget)? File request, get okay (CCB = approval group), update plans. No skipping!

    Ex: Client wants faster—request, don’t rush.

  • Quality: Make it good. Check work (QC = inspect), fix how you work (QA = process). Tools: Pareto (80% problems from 20% causes), Ishikawa (why it broke), Gantt (task bars).

    Ex: App crashes—Pareto finds top bugs.

  • Buying Stuff: Fixed-price (set $, seller risks), Cost-plus (you pay extras), T&M (hourly). Delay? Who’s stuck pays.

    Ex: Fixed-price coder late—they fix, not you.

  • Tools:
    • WBS: Task breakdown (e.g., app = design, code, test).
    • Gantt: Bar chart of tasks over time.
    • Channels: n(n-1)/2 (5 people = 10 lines).

Exam Trick: Math (10 Qs) = EVM, channels. Agile (40+) = no mid-changes. Always file changes.

3. Business (8% - About 15 Questions)

Why We’re Doing This

Small but key—links project to company goals and rules.

  • Match Company Goals: Project must help (e.g., make $). Goals shift? Check impact, file change—don’t just quit or keep going.

    Ex: Sales drop—see if app still fits, adjust.

  • Payoff: What’s the win (cash, happy users)? Write it (business case), check after.

    Ex: App saves time—that’s the win.

  • Rules: Be fair, honest (PMI Code). Qs test right vs. wrong—pick right.

    Ex: Vendor offers cash—say no, tell boss.

Exam Trick: Ethics Qs (5-10) = always honest. Goal shift = assess.

4. Pass Plan (2 Weeks to Certified)

Learn This, Win

No experience, no耐心—this gets you there fast. 2 weeks, 1-2 hrs/day.

  • Week 1 - Learn (1 Hr/Day):
    • Days 1-2: People (30 Qs): Conflicts (talk), team (help), stakeholders (update)—20 min each.
    • Days 3-6: Process (60 Qs): EVM (6 formulas, 20 min), agile (sprints, 20 min), risk (EMV, 10 min), change (file, 10 min).
    • Day 7: Business (15 Qs): Goals (assess), ethics (honest)—30 min.

    Memorize: Write these 15 lines daily (10 min):

    • CV = EV - AC, + = good
    • SV = EV - PV, + = fast
    • CPI = EV / AC, >1 = good
    • SPI = EV / PV, >1 = fast
    • EAC = BAC / CPI
    • TCPI = (BAC - EV) / (BAC - AC)
    • Critical path = longest, no delays
    • Sprint = 2-4 weeks, no mid-changes
    • Daily Scrum = 15 min
    • Product Owner = goals, team = picks
    • Risk EMV = chance × cost
    • Risk fix = avoid, mitigate, transfer, accept
    • Change = file request, CCB
    • Pareto = 80/20
    • Ethics = honest, fair

  • Week 2 - Practice (1-2 Hrs/Day):
    • Days 8-11: Do 300 Qs (use my 500: Q1-Q50 here, Q113-Q500 prior). 75 Qs/day, 1-2 hrs. Aim 70% (210/300). Review wrongs—10 min each.
    • Day 12: Mini Test (below), 25 Qs, 30 min.
    • Days 13-14: Review weak spots (EVM, agile), redo 50 Qs/day.
  • Mini Test (25 Qs):
    • 1. Team fights—Talk it out? Yes (b).
    • 2. Slow coder—Help first? Yes (b).
    • 3. Client wants update—Plan it? Yes (b).
    • 4. EV = $100K, AC = $110K, CV = ? -$10K (b).
    • 5. EV = $80K, PV = $100K, SPI = ? 0.8 (a).
    • 6. Mid-sprint add—Backlog it? Yes (b).
    • 7. Risk 20% × $50K = ? $10K (c).
    • 8. Scope tweak—File request? Yes (b).
    • 9. Pareto does what? Prioritizes (b).
    • 10. Fixed-price delay—Vendor fixes? Yes (b).
    • 11. Goals shift—Assess it? Yes (b).
    • 12-25: Mix from my 500 (Q13, Q22, etc.).
  • Test Day: 76 sec/Q, guess if stuck (no penalty). PMI loves process—pick “talk, plan, file” over “ignore, rush.”

Surefire: Learn 15 lines, practice 300 Qs—2 weeks, you’ll hit ~122/175!
